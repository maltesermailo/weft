  What landed ‚ÄĒ the full ¬ß2.4 ladder (invariant 9)

  You picked "both, recovery first," so this is the recovery half done as a
  complete unit:

  - Rung 1 ‚ÄĒ NS TRANSFER (no delay): root-key-signed succession. This is the
  moment the namespace root key we've been storing since M4-5 finally does
  cryptographic work ‚ÄĒ same-network namespace authority is now genuinely
  enforced by a signature, not just a table. Forged sigs ‚Üí FORBIDDEN.
  - Rung 2 ‚ÄĒ social quorum (7-day window): NS RECOVERY SET designates an M-of-N
  quorum; NS RECOVER with ‚Č• m quorum co-signatures starts a delayed, announced
  recovery.
  - Rung 3 ‚ÄĒ operator last resort (30-day window): the same verb,
  network-key-signed, permanently marked operator-initiated in root-history.
  - NS RECOVERY CANCEL ‚ÄĒ a root-signed veto; a live root always wins.
  - Scheduled application ‚ÄĒ a background task rotates the key + owner at the
  delay's expiry and writes the audit trail.

  How invariant 9 holds ‚ÄĒ "no silent root rotation path"

  Every root change is gated: TRANSFER needs a signature, RECOVER only ever 
  starts a delayed+announced pending state (never rotates immediately),
  application happens only in the scheduler after the delay and always records
  root-history, and CANCEL gives a live root the veto. The crypto lives in a new
  pure weft-crypto::rotation module with three domain-separated signed
  statements ‚ÄĒ a test proves you can't manufacture a quorum by padding
  signatures with duplicates or outsiders, and tampering invalidates everything.
  The application logic is a standalone apply_due_recoveries so it's
  unit-tested at a far-future clock without waiting real days.

  One honest gap (in the review + spec)

  The recovery announcement is queryable on NS-META
  (recovery=pending;recovery-eta=;recovery-rung=) but not yet pushed to all
  members ‚ÄĒ namespaces don't have a member-roster broadcast channel yet. That's
  a delivery gap, not an enforcement gap: all the invariant-9 guarantees (no
  silent path, cancellable window, permanent operator marking) hold. Flagged in
  reviews/m4c-recovery-ladder-review.md rather than glossed over.

  Now the second half of what you asked for ‚ÄĒ reporting + retention holds
  (invariants 11 & 12: reported events exempt from purge/compaction, reporter
  confidentiality) ‚ÄĒ is the last M4 piece. It's self-contained and touches the
  M3 purge/compaction paths. Ready to continue into it whenever you'd like, or
  this is a clean checkpoint (M4a + M4b + recovery all done, 207 tests) if you
  want to pause.
